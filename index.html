I'm Syed Talib Haider. You can literally call me any one of those 3 names. Ever since I started graduation in 2020, I have done internships and projects in web writing. Then, I got my big break in 2021, and that started my journey of 5 years in high-paced newsrooms. I remember when I wrote my first article, I still couldn't believe seeing it published on the frontend. But since then, there's been no looking back. Climbed into the editor's chair, then took on content management. Multiple hats over the years. But one thing stayed key the whole time: the writing, and understanding the audience psychology so that I adapt my art according to the space it's going to live in.

Audience psychology is the thing people overlook. But writing is only worth it if you know the channels that you can sell it in. Just like a piece of art. See, Banksy, he knows where to place his art and how to do that. He has an art piece for every location. That's a good artist. Similarly, a good writer knows how to write different pieces for different audiences. It's important to double down on what's working and bin what isn't via tracking traffic and trends. My time as an Affiliate Content Producer at The Sporting News added a brand layer to that. Content has to rank AND convert revenue, and the audience psychology underneath is the same one that fuels good brand strategy.

Then comes AI integration. I'll be honest, many traditionalists are still skeptical for good reason. Growth-first newsrooms learned to use the tools. I sit firmly in the second camp. Built a Strategic AI Stack around Gemini and Claude for the heavy lifting: trend extraction, competitor scans, fact-check. The machine handles the heavy lifting, while I fry my brain wholly and solely on narration. AI can't replace the storyteller, but it will kill the average writer scrambling to position his work.

Outside the desk: cinephile. Preminger, Hitchcock, Wilder, Kubrick, Scorsese, Spielberg. I've rinsed the canon and I keep going back. Liverpool on weekends. Reading when the slump lifts. A self-published poetry collection, Almost, Always, sitting on Pothi and Lulu.
